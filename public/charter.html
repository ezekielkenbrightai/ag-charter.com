Service Charter

Office of the Attorney General & Department of Justice

Our Mandate

Established under Article 156 of the Constitution of Kenya and the Office of the Attorney General Act (Cap 6A), the OAG serves as the Government's Principal Legal Advisor, responsible for legal representation, human rights protection, anti-corruption, and legal aid initiatives.

Our Vision

To be the best institution in the region in the provision of public legal services and promotion of a just, democratic and corruption-free nation.

Our Mission

To facilitate the realization of good governance and respect for the rule of law through the provision of public legal services, protection and promotion of human rights and upholding of ethics and integrity.

Core Values

Professionalism Integrity Transparency & Accountability Efficiency & Effectiveness Equity & Fairness Teamwork Courtesy & Customer Focus Non-discrimination

Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs)

Uniform procedures ensuring consistent, auditable service delivery
SOP-001

Case Intake & Assignment

Civil Litigation | International Law
  1. Receipt: Written instructions received from MDA via ICMS or official correspondence. Logged by Registry within 1 working day.
  2. Triage: Head of Department reviews case within 2 days, classifies priority (Critical / High / Medium / Low) and assigns to State Counsel.
  3. Acknowledgement: Assigned counsel acknowledges receipt and sends written confirmation to instructing MDA within 3 days of assignment.
  4. Case Plan: Counsel prepares case strategy memo within 7 days, submitting to supervisor for approval.
  5. Monitoring: Case tracked on ICMS with milestones. Quarterly review by Critical Litigation Committee for high-priority cases.
  6. Closure: Upon resolution, counsel files closure report. Case archived digitally. Client satisfaction survey sent to MDA.
Total SLA: Initial response within 14 days of instruction
SOP-002

Government Contract Vetting

Government Transactions
  1. Submission: MDA submits draft contract with all procurement documentation via workflow system. Registry logs within 1 day.
  2. Completeness Check: Registry verifies all required documents received within 2 days. Returns incomplete files with checklist.
  3. Legal Review: Assigned counsel reviews contract for legal compliance, risk, and value-for-money within 7 working days.
  4. Quality Assurance: Senior counsel / HOD reviews draft opinion within 3 working days.
  5. Clearance / Advisory: Legal clearance certificate issued, or advisory letter with recommended amendments sent to MDA.
  6. Follow-Up: If amendments required, MDA resubmits. Amended contract reviewed within 5 working days.
Total SLA: 14 working days (complete submission)
SOP-003

Marriage Registration (Civil)

Registrar General
  1. Application: Parties attend Registrar's office or apply via e-Citizen. Application form completed with supporting documents.
  2. Document Verification: Registrar verifies IDs, confirms eligibility (age, capacity, no existing marriage). Same day.
  3. Notice Publication: Marriage notice posted for 21 days. Any objections investigated within the notice period.
  4. Ceremony: Marriage solemnized by Registrar in the presence of 2 witnesses. Marriage register signed.
  5. Certificate Issuance: Marriage certificate issued on the day of ceremony or collected within 3 working days.
  6. Digitization: Marriage record entered into digital registry within 1 working day. Available for certified copy requests.
Total SLA: 21 days (notice) + 1 day (ceremony)
SOP-004

Legal Aid Application Processing

National Legal Aid Service (NLAS)
  1. Intake: Applicant visits Justice Centre, Sheria Mtaani clinic, or applies online. Intake officer records details and documents.
  2. Means Test: Eligibility assessed based on income, assets, and vulnerability criteria within 3 working days.
  3. Merit Assessment: Legal officer reviews case merit (reasonable prospects of success, public interest) within 5 working days.
  4. Approval: NLAS Approving Officer makes determination. Approved applicants notified within 2 days.
  5. Counsel Assignment: State Counsel or accredited provider assigned. Introduction meeting scheduled within 7 days of approval.
  6. Case Management: Regular progress updates to beneficiary. Case tracked on ICMS. Closure report filed upon resolution.
Total SLA: 7 working days (intake to assignment)
SOP-005

Advocates Complaint Investigation

Advocates Complaints Commission
  1. Receipt: Complaint received in writing (physical/online). Acknowledged within 3 working days with reference number.
  2. Screening: ACC Secretariat screens complaint for jurisdiction and prima facie merit within 7 days.
  3. IHADR Offer: Both parties offered In-House ADR. If accepted, mediation scheduled within 14 days.
  4. Investigation: If IHADR declined or fails, formal investigation commenced. Advocate notified and given 14 days to respond.
  5. Investigation Report: Investigating officer completes report within 60 days of commencement.
  6. Prosecution / Closure: If charges warranted, filed before Disciplinary Tribunal within 14 days. If not, complainant notified of closure with reasons.
Total SLA: 90 days (complaint to determination)
SOP-006

Business / Company Registration

Business Registration Service
  1. Name Search: Applicant submits proposed name via e-Citizen. BRS conducts name availability search within 1 working day.
  2. Name Reservation: Approved name reserved for 30 days. Applicant notified electronically.
  3. Document Submission: Applicant uploads memorandum, articles, directors' details, KRA PINs via BRS portal.
  4. Compliance Review: BRS officer verifies documents for Companies Act compliance within 2 working days.
  5. Certificate Issuance: Certificate of Incorporation generated electronically and delivered via e-Citizen portal.
  6. Post-Registration: Company details published. KRA, NHIF, NSSF cross-registration facilitated.
Total SLA: 3-5 working days
SOP-007

Legislative Drafting Process

Legislative Drafting Department
  1. Policy Receipt: Approved Cabinet memo / policy brief received from sponsoring MDA. Logged within 1 day.
  2. Drafting Brief: Chief Parliamentary Counsel assigns drafting counsel. Drafting brief meeting with MDA within 5 days.
  3. First Draft: Counsel prepares draft Bill within 21 working days, ensuring Constitutional and legal consistency.
  4. Inter-Ministerial Review: Draft circulated to relevant MDAs for comment. 14-day comment window.
  5. Revision & Finalization: Comments incorporated, final draft prepared. Quality review by senior counsel.
  6. Submission: Final Bill with Memorandum of Objects submitted to AG for approval and onward transmission to Parliament.
Total SLA: 30-60 working days
SOP-008

Estate Administration (Public Trustee)

Public Trustee's Office
  1. Application: Next of kin or interested party applies to Public Trustee with death certificate and relationship proof.
  2. Asset Investigation: Public Trustee identifies and documents all assets and liabilities within 30 days.
  3. Grant Application: Petition for grant of letters of administration filed in court. Hearing within court schedule.
  4. Asset Collection: Upon grant, Trustee collects assets, notifies debtors and creditors. Creditors given 90 days to file claims.
  5. Distribution: After settling debts, assets distributed per succession law or will. Distribution account filed with court.
  6. Closure: Estate file closed upon completion. Beneficiaries issued final accounting statement.
Total SLA: 90-180 days (simple estate)
SOP-009

Legal Opinion Issuance

Legal Advisory & Research
  1. Request Receipt: Written request from MDA received and logged. Completeness check within 1 day.
  2. Assignment: HOD assigns research counsel within 2 days based on subject expertise.
  3. Research: Counsel researches applicable law, comparative analysis, and precedents within 7 working days.
  4. Draft Opinion: Written legal opinion drafted with clear recommendation within 3 working days of research completion.
  5. Review & Approval: Senior counsel reviews for quality and consistency. Submitted to SG/AG for signature.
  6. Dispatch: Signed opinion dispatched to requesting MDA. Copy filed digitally.
Total SLA: 14 working days (standard); 7 days (urgent)
SOP-010

Workflow Approval Process (Digital)

All Departments (Cross-Cutting)
  1. Submission: Initiator submits request via OAG Workflow System with all supporting documents attached.
  2. Auto-Routing: System routes to appropriate reviewer based on request type and value thresholds.
  3. Level 1 Review: Assigned officer reviews within 3 working days. Approves, returns for correction, or escalates.
  4. Level 2 Review: Senior officer / HOD reviews within 2 working days for quality assurance.
  5. Final Approval: SG or AG approves within 2 working days. Digital signature applied.
  6. Notification: All parties notified of outcome. Audit trail logged automatically.
Total SLA: 7 working days (standard); 3 days (urgent)
